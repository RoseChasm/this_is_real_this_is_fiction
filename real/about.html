about me

My name is Michaela Stone Cross (that's pronounced "Mick-I-luh," and yes I know that's not a real name but it's the only one I've got...) I've spent much of my life torn between loving art and politics and recently decided not to choose.

I recently graduated from the University of Chicago concentrating in South Asian Studies. I'm interested in international/national politics, in the American justice system, and violence towards women. I've written a play based on an actual trial in Philadelphia, the story of a lawyer fighting to get a not-guilty for a man who is very much so. I've written an article about sexual assault I faced/observed in India, one that went internationally viral, and got me on the news. I've interned at Politico and NPR, and built a book into the internet.

I'm a strong and proudly subjective writer, and my goal is to figure out a way I can give a voice to people who need to borrow my own. Growing up as the daughter of a Philadelphia public defender the stories of people enslaved to our country's broken justice system were always part of my daily life. Even as I studied ancient Greek and Indian philosophies at an elite university, the stories my father taught me remained close to me, and I've seen my education as a short respite from the struggles I want to be immersed in.

I think art should not be estranged from politics, and so I have and intend to continue to make art that is a part of the world instead of something shimmering above it. I think the key to the struggles art faces is to be accessable to a varied public. And so I built this website as a platform not just for my art but for others' art too.
